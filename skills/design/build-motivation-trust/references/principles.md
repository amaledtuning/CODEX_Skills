# Motivation and Trust Principles

Source anchor: paraphrased from the design book in `D:\Project Skills\Книги по дизайну\100_glavnyh_principov_dizayna_kak_uderzhat_vnimanie.pdf`, especially motivation, low-friction paths, social behavior, emotion, memory, trust, and story sections around pp. 141-219.

## Core Principles

- Motivation increases when progress is visible. People are more likely to continue when they can see movement toward a goal.
- The closer people feel to completion, the more motivated they become. Use progress cues honestly and proportionally.
- Make the intended path easy. Low friction supports adoption more reliably than persuasion alone.
- Rewards work best when they reinforce meaningful progress, not when they distract from the user's goal.
- Curiosity and surprise can increase attention, but they should reveal value, not obscure it.
- People look to other people for signals. Social proof, community evidence, testimonials, and human presence can increase trust when credible.
- Emotion improves memory. Stories, faces, sensory detail, and specific consequences make material easier to remember than raw facts alone.
- Trust depends on clarity, control, consistency, and credible proof.

## Practical Checklist

- Define what the user needs to believe before continuing.
- Show specific value before asking for effort or commitment.
- Add progress only when it represents real progress.
- Use social proof that is concrete and relevant to the user's context.
- Show human cues where they reduce uncertainty: faces, names, roles, authorship, support access, or customer context.
- Use reassurance near risk points: payment, submission, deletion, sharing, or personal data.
- Remove unnecessary friction before adding persuasive copy.
- Give users control: clear exits, editable choices, and transparent consequences.
- For decks and PDFs, support claims with credible evidence and a memorable story arc.
- For images/posters, align emotion, subject, and call to action.

## Anti-Patterns

- Fake scarcity, fake countdowns, or exaggerated urgency.
- Social proof without context or credibility.
- Progress indicators that reset, mislead, or hide remaining effort.
- Emotional imagery unrelated to the actual value proposition.
- Asking for trust before explaining consequences.
- Hiding controls to increase conversion.

## Output Pattern

When using this skill, produce:

1. Desired belief or behavior.
2. Motivation/trust blockers.
3. Ethical cues to add.
4. Friction to remove.
5. Dark patterns to avoid.
6. Medium-specific notes for UI, deck, PDF, image, poster, ad, checkout, or product story.
